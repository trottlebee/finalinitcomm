Crowning Glory

100 Views

If a woman has long hair, it is a glory to her: for her hair is given her for a covering.


I CORINTHIANS 11:15


We, as humans, have so much untapped power. We tend to be easily discouraged, however. When life begins to trouble us, as it often does, we forget that we’ve been crowned with glory. And not only glory, but protection and provision from God.

The human heart is oft spoken of in scripture. It features three parts—will, intellect, and emotion. Our responsibility is to devote ourselves to transforming our hearts (or minds) for the better:

[B]e not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God. Romans 12:2

Jesus affirmed that we should devote the fullness of our being to God:

Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy strength, and with all thy mind …. Luke 10:27

When we follow His instructions, we empower ourselves greatly. As an example, let’s consider the power of discernment. The more we study the scriptures and apply God’s timeless precepts and commands to our lives, the better we are at guarding our minds and protecting our hearts from worldly allurement:

[W]hen for the time ye ought to be teachers, ye have need that one teach you again which be the first principles of the oracles of God; and are become such as have need of milk, and not of strong meat. For every one that useth milk is unskillful in the word of righteousness: for he is a babe. But strong meat belongeth to them that are of full age, even those who by reason of use have their senses exercised to discern both good and evil. Hebrews 5:12-14

Consider how often you have to make decisions during an average day. Whenever those decisions align with God’s will, don’t you find that they are advantageous? The more I age, the more I find this to be true. I make better financial decisions, relationship decisions, and family decisions when I’m in alignment with the Lord’s perfect will.

Try to keep in mind, God has our best interest at heart at all times. He wants us to have a fruitful existence during our earthly journey. His precepts and commands help us build a firm foundation for making sound, discerning decisions for ourselves, our families, our very lives.

Finally, as we build this foundation for empowerment, God expects us to protect it:

Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it. Proverbs 4:23

Living with a mind for Christ puts amazing power within our reach. Begin tapping into your power today ….
