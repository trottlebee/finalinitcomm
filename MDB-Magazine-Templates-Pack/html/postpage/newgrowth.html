Out of Trying Circumstances Growth Results

100 Views

My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience. But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing.


JAMES 1:2-4


We tend to want carefree lives. But carefree lives are fragile and fleeting. We are truly strengthened and empowered by the trials we face. The scripture above attests to the edifying nature of trying circumstances. Trials build and edify our patience.

Our trials are also tailor-made to us. We are all products of our individual experiences. God, our omniscient creator and sustainer, knows what each person needs to be reconciled to Him in the end. As such, He sends us challenges to rid ourselves of whatever hinders us spiritually:

If your hand causes you to sin, cut it off. It is better for you to enter into life maimed, rather than having two hands, to go to hell, into the fire that shall never be quenched where ‘Their worm does not die and the fire is not quenched.’ And if your foot causes you to sin, cut it off. It is better for you to enter life lame, rather than having two feet, to be cast into hell …. And if your eye causes you to sin, pluck it out. It is better for you to enter the kingdom of God with one eye, rather than having two eyes, to be cast into hell fire .... Mark 9:43-45, 47

The next time you find yourself facing a trial, try to remember: the Lord is the potter (Jeremiah 18:6, Isaiah 64:8). He is constantly chipping away at everything in us that is contrary to His will. When we let Him work His work in us, we become His spiritual masterpieces. Empowered to serve as role models of faith for those watching—empowered to be steadfast in reaching ever-higher goals, both material and spiritual.
